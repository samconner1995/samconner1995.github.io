A fast-paced hybrid of card battler and action game for up to 8 players! Fight as a mech, then hop into your base to deploy your troops to the battlefield! Fend off the Blue Invasion in Co-op Mode, or fight your friends in Battle Mode!

My Role

  • Programmer
  • Game Designer

AI Behavior

Through use of steering vectors and finite state machines, dynamic behavior is achieved! Computer players look at their internal state and the environment around them, and calculate their desired movement vectors and actions in real time.

Procedural Generation

Using primarily weighted randomness, a diverse set of enemy armies, characters, equipment and upgrade choices are generated at the start of each run. The players navigate through these choices in between battles. Each playthrough is different!

Data collection

Data is collected in the background in real time during battles, players' damage dealt, damage received, and their efficiency as a commander are among the data collected. This is then processed and shown to the players after each battle. Players love validating their strategies through data!

Animation System

Each player's body is broken up into 6 pieces. These pieces are animated independently via custom animation code. Players' legs run, sidestep, and backpeddle. The force of firing a gun travels from the gun through the arms to the shoulders.

8 player input management

Up to 8 players can play locally. Keyboard and gamepads are supported. Using the OpenXInput API, 8 XINPUT devices can be used simultaneously, bypassing API's limit of 4.

3d Card effects (UI)

Using surfaces, shaders, and some math, a 3d effect is created for the playing cards. Small UI touches like this go a long way!

Online Play

Online play is achieved through the Steamworks API. The game state is synced using an authoritative server that validates requests from clients before sending approval.
